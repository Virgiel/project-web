Conway's Game of Life • About

Principle

Conway’s Game of Life is a zero-player game with a cellular automaton created by the British mathematician John Horton Conway. The game of Life is played on an infinite two-dimensional rectangular grid of cells. Each cell can be either alive or dead depending on its 8 neighbors. A pattern is called a generation and it evolves from applying the rules simultaneously to every cell on the game board. One interacts with the Game of Life by creating an initial configuration and observing how it evolves.

Rules

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies (underpopulation).
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies (overpopulation).
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives, unchanged, to the next generation.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors will come to life.

Origins

John von Neumann, a renowned mathematician, conceived cellular automaton. He failed at creating automatons that could build copies of itself. He works on this problem in a purely mathematical way by studying how a self-reproduction process can be simulated on a grid where each cell can only have a limited number of states. These works will be published in his posthumous work Theory of Self-Reproductive Automata. He inspired John Conway on the Game of Life. Conways tried to simplify von Neumann’s idea. The game was first published on October 1970.

Howard Gardner, a developmental psychologist, wrote:
"The game made Conway instantly famous, but it also opened up a whole new field of mathematical research, the field of cellular automata... Because of Life's analogies with the rise, fall and alterations of a society of living organisms, it belongs to a growing class of what are called 'simulation games' (games that resemble real life processes)"

Simulation

  • Speed
    You can change the speed of the life simulation and observe a faster evolution.
  • CellSize
    You can change cells sizes and create simulation whit a bigger or smaller population.
  • Pause/Play
    To pause or play the simulation, you can click on the Play/Pause button or you can also hit Space.
  • Random
    To create a new random initial configuration, you can click on the Random button.
